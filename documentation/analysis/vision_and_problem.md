# Project Vision & Problem Statement: AttendanceFlow-AMS

This document captures the "Why" behind the project, rooted in the Design Thinking process.

## 🎯 The Core Problem

The primary bottleneck identified is the **manual, paper-based attendance flow**.
- **The "Double Entry" Friction**: Teachers mark paper in class, then someone (usually an Admin) must manually type those records into Excel.
- **Granularity Loss**: Paper records often miss the specific session time (e.g., 9-11 vs 11-14), making accurate reporting difficult.
- **Latency**: Administration only sees attendance days or weeks after the fact.

## 🔭 The Vision

To create a **unified, real-time ecosystem** where:
1. **Data is captured once** at the source (the classroom).
2. **Admins verify** instead of typing.
3. **Students are informed** automatically about their status.

---

## 🔬 Design Thinking Stages

### Phase 1: Empathy
Understanding the daily struggles of teachers (Imane), admins (Hannane), and students. The system is designed to serve their specific pain points.

### Phase 2: Define (The HMW Questions)
- **HMW** eliminate the physical transfer of paper?
- **HMW** allow teachers to mark a full class in **under 30 seconds**?
- **HMW** digitize the submission of medical justifications?

### Phase 3: Ideation
- **Solution A**: Direct mobile-first entry.
- **Solution B**: A "Validation Hub" for admins to oversee real-time data.
- **Solution C**: Automated alerts for at-risk students.

---

## 📈 Success Metrics
- **Time Reduction**: Attendance entry should take < 30 seconds.
- **Zero Paper**: 100% elimination of paper attendance sheets.
- **Data Accuracy**: Real-time synchronization between teacher entry and admin view.
